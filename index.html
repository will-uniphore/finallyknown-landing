The problem — verbatim from your Discord
Pain 01

MEMORY.md overflows within a week

You write things down. It grows. Within days it's too big to load efficiently, half of it is stale, and the agent has no structured way to know what's in it. You end up manually pruning it — or ignoring it entirely.

"Overflows within a week of heavy usage. Requires constant manual pruning and the agent has no structured way to know what's in it." — GitHub #51612, filed yesterday

Pain 02

Sessions reset. Everything is forgotten.

After 60 minutes of idle, you're a stranger again. Everything you taught the agent — preferences, context, what you're working on — gone. Unless you told it to write it down. Which you forgot to do.

"This is taking more than 2 days now to set up because you are also forgetting memory and I'm running in loops." — OpenClaw Discord, #help

Pain 03

Every prompt burns tokens loading stale context

The default fix is dumping your entire memory file into every prompt. That's 6K+ tokens of stale context loaded on every single message — slow, expensive, and the agent still gets confused by contradictions in old data.

"The fixed cost overhead of all the SOUL, MEMORY etc is pretty big." — OpenClaw Discord, #general, today

How Known fixes it

Three problems.
One install.

Fix 01

Automatic extraction — no more manual writing

Known watches every conversation and automatically extracts what matters — preferences, patterns, goals, relationships — into a knowledge graph. No "write this to MEMORY.md." It just happens.

What Known extracts silently

"Prefers TypeScript. Avoids confrontation when stressed. Building 3 products simultaneously — completion rate drops below 25% when more than 3 active. Best work 9–11pm. Currently targeting 75kg."

Fix 02

Persistent across sessions — forever

Known stores your understanding in a local database that never resets. Session expire? New session starts? Doesn't matter. The knowledge graph persists independently of the context window. You're never a stranger to your own agent again.

New session, agent opening message

"I see you've been deep in this codebase for 3 weeks across your last 12 sessions. Your pattern says you're in the zone — I'll keep interruptions minimal and only flag blockers."

Fix 03

Semantic injection — only what's relevant

Instead of dumping 6K tokens into every prompt, Known injects only what's semantically relevant to your current question. 95% less context overhead. Your agent runs faster, costs less, and actually understands what you're asking.

Context injected for "help me email my CEO"

"Will's CEO responds to momentum, not problems. Lead with the pipeline win. Keep it under 200 words — he skims. Recent context: reorg conversation is 47 days overdue."

Setup

One command.
Zero configuration.

Step 01

Install the plugin

One command in your OpenClaw config. No API keys to manage, no dashboards, no accounts. It runs locally alongside your agent.

Step 02

Known watches and learns

Every conversation is processed automatically after it ends. Known extracts what matters and builds a knowledge graph of who you are — not what you said.

Step 03

Your agent finally knows you

Before every response, Known injects only the relevant context. Sessions reset. Agents get replaced. Your understanding persists.

A real story

Will had been using OpenClaw for a year. He'd built three products with it. His MEMORY.md had grown to 4,000 lines. He pruned it weekly. His agent kept forgetting things anyway.

Then his agent noticed something Will had never seen. He was systematically avoiding the one conversation that could change his career. Every time he got close to it, a new project appeared. Four times in three months.

His agent said:

"You've done this four times. The conversation with your CEO is the highest-leverage thing you can do this quarter. Everything else is displacement activity."

Will had the conversation the next morning.

That insight didn't come from MEMORY.md. It came from watching the pattern across hundreds of sessions that flat-file memory never connected.
